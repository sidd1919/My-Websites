Section 02 — Origin Story
The Making of a Champion
5 NOV 1988
Born in Delhi, India
Born to Prem Kohli (a criminal lawyer) and Saroj Kohli in Uttam Nagar, West Delhi. Raised in a middle-class household with an elder brother Vikas and sister Bhavna.
1994
First cricket bat at age 3
Kohli's father gifted him a cricket bat at the age of three, noticing his unusual fascination with the sport. He would mimic batting stances obsessively.
1998
Enrolled at West Delhi Cricket Academy
Joined the Academy under coach Rajkumar Sharma at age 9. Showed exceptional talent — hard-working, disciplined and technically sound from a very young age.
2002
Delhi Under-15 Selection
Selected for Delhi U-15 team, performing consistently in youth tournaments. His hunger to improve set him apart from peers.
18 DEC 2006
Father passed away — he played next morning
His father Prem Kohli died of a brain stroke. Kohli played in the Ranji Trophy match against Karnataka the very next morning, scoring 90, then rushed for the last rites. This moment defined his character.
AI
"This year changed everything." No single act defined Kohli's mental fortitude more than playing Ranji cricket within 24 hours of losing his father — a widely cited testament to his professionalism and resolve.
2008
Led India to U-19 World Cup victory
Captained India's Under-19 team to World Cup triumph in Malaysia, cementing his status as a future superstar and earning him the captaincy mantle early.

Section 03 — Professional Journey
A Career Told in Phases
2008–11
EMERGENCE

The Young Maverick

Kohli made his ODI debut on August 18, 2008, against Sri Lanka. Initially raw and inconsistent, his aggressive style divided opinion. He struggled for form but showed flashes of brilliance. Selected for 2011 ODI World Cup squad as the team's youngest member.

ODI debut 2008 Test debut 2011 World Cup 2011 winner First ODI century 2008
Pivotal moment
2011–14
RISE

The Run Machine Emerges

Post-2011 World Cup, Kohli transformed into India's most reliable batter across formats. His ODI average soared above 50, and he emerged as the successor to Sachin Tendulkar's legacy. The 2012 Asia Cup against Sri Lanka (183 runs off 148 balls) announced his arrival on the world stage.

183 vs Sri Lanka (2012) 3 consecutive ODI centuries ICC ODI Cricketer 2012 First Test ton at Adelaide
2014–19
DOMINANCE

Captain. Champion. Conqueror.

Named Test captain in 2014, then full captain across all formats by 2017. Under his leadership, India rose to No. 1 in Tests, won 40 of 65 Tests. He became the fastest to 10,000 ODI runs and scored 50 international centuries. Arguably the best batter in all three formats simultaneously.

Test captain 2014 India #1 Test ranking ICC No.1 batter (all formats) Wisden Cricketer of Decade 2010s 10,000 ODI runs fastest
AI
"The peak that redefined greatness." From 2016–2019, Kohli averaged 77.66 in ODIs and 59.80 in Tests simultaneously — a cross-format dominance with no modern parallel in cricket history.
2019–22
TRANSITION

The Drought & the Return

A century drought lasting 1020 days tested Kohli's resolve. He stepped down as Test captain in January 2022, ended his T20I captaincy after the 2021 T20 World Cup. Critics questioned his form — but Kohli had weathered storms before.

Centurion drought 2019–22 Captaincy relinquished Scored 71st ton (Asia Cup 2022)
2022–25
LEGACY

The King Reclaims the Throne

The comeback was emphatic. 50th ODI century in 2023, T20 World Cup winner in 2024, ICC Men's T20I Cricketer of the Year 2023. In the 2023 ODI World Cup, Kohli was the tournament's highest scorer with 765 runs including 3 hundreds — the best individual World Cup batting performance in history.
